1. The Strategy: When to Use What
Think of your website like a restaurant. Your Pages are the building (address, menu, about us), while your Posts are the "Specials" board that changes every day.

Feature	Pages (The Static Bones)	Posts (The Dynamic Muscle)
Purpose	Evergreen, foundational info.	Timely, social, and searchable content.
Organization	Hierarchical (Parent/Child).	Categories and Tags.
Lifespan	Long-term (rarely changes).	Short-to-medium term (regularly updated).
MVP Goal	Establish credibility & "the offer."	Drive traffic & test market interest.
2. Creating Your MVP "Core" (Pages)
For an MVP, you only need 3 to 4 pages. Don't overbuild.

Home: Your "Hook." Use a simple Call to Action (CTA) block immediately at the top.

About: Who are you and why should they trust you? Keep it under 300 words.

Contact/Sign-up: A simple form or email address. This is your "Conversion" metric for the MVP.

Privacy Policy: Use the WordPress "Auto-generate" tool to stay legal with zero effort.

MVP Tip: Use the Block Patterns (pre-designed layouts) inside the editor. Don't waste hours designing from scratch; pick a "Services" or "About" pattern and just swap the text.

3. Creating Your MVP "Growth" (Blog/Posts)
Posts are your MVP’s secret weapon for SEO and Validation.

Categories vs. Tags:

Categories: Broad buckets (e.g., "Product Updates," "Industry News"). Stick to 3 max for an MVP.

Tags: Specific keywords (e.g., "Tutorial," "2026 Trends"). Use these sparingly.

The "Latest Posts" Page: You don't need to design this. In Settings > Reading, set your "Posts page" to a blank page named "Blog." WordPress will automatically feed your new posts there in reverse-chronological order.

4. The MVP Workflow (The "Quick-Start" List)
Appearance > Themes: Choose a lightweight "Block Theme" like Twenty Twenty-Five.

Pages > Add New: Create your Home, About, and Contact pages.

Posts > Add New: Write one "Hello World" style post explaining your MVP’s mission.

Appearance > Menus: (Or "Navigation" in the Site Editor) Drag your Pages into the top bar.

Settings > Permalinks: Set to "Post name." This is crucial for MVP SEO—it makes your URLs look like site.com/my-awesome-post instead of site.com/?p=123.
